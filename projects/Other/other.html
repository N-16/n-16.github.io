Dots and Boxes

The project made for an AI vs AI battle against a friend, is based on a pen-paper game. The game starts with an empty grid of dots. Two players take turns by adding a single horizontal or vertical line between two adjacent dots. The player who completes the fourth side of a box earns one point and takes another turn. The game ends when the grid is full and the player completing the most number of boxes wins.

This game is played in the single-player mode, in which the player plays against the AI.
